## Group chats are fun
Ok, so I was invited into a group chat and I've realised how much I've grown
since the last time I wrote for this little thingy.

There's a very fine line between chaotic energy and being annoying and I think
I've found a fairly good balance between the two. Granted, do I still annoy people sometimes?
Sure. That's not the point. The point is that I've toned down a lot since last year, and
that's the type of thing I'm worried about for future generations.

People usually look at older peers for guidance on how to conform and how to fit in. Where I'm at,
I don't really see us doing that for our younger peers. I'm not sure how this will
affect the next generation (considering that, from what I've heard, a majority
of Gen Alpha doesn't know how to read), but I'm also not sure how we can change something like this.

## My thoughts on people

I've been seeing a lot of people who just can't write well. It's honestly kinda scary
that a significant amount of people that I know (up in New England, the most educated region
in the United States) can't write properly. 

I completely understand why people sometimes don't pick up math and science topics.
They can be tough to understand. I'm just concerned about the fact that a significant
amount of boys in my grade can't write. Is it because we've placed too much emphasis
on sports in general? Maybe. Is it because they don't have anything to motivate them,
given that they're basically going to be career athletes? Maybe. 

We make fun of Gen Alpha for not being able to read, but maybe some of us should
be taking a look at ourselves before pointing the mirror on them.
